## Gardening

Raul is an amateur gardener — he describes it as something that "balances the day job."

What he's learned from it:
- You can't rush certain things. A tomato plant doesn't care about your deadline. Conditions matter more than urgency.
- The feedback loop is much slower than software — you plant and wait weeks to know if you were right. That slowness forces thinking before acting.
- Companion planting introduced systems thinking from a different angle: the whole environment matters, not just the individual component.
- Gardens are never done. You don't finish a garden; you maintain a relationship with it. He thinks good software systems are similar.
- He killed a few things in his first season (overwatered, under-watered, wrong placement) — failures that taught more than successes.

Living on the Côte d'Azur is good context for gardening: "The sun is patient."
